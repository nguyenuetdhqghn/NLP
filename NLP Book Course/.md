# Welcome to my NLP class notes

These class notes are for use with Jupyter notebooks in the NLP course. NLP is a trending field and requires a range of skills, most of which we will touch upon in the material for this course. See [NLP  skills](https://opendatascience.com/20-nlp-platforms-and-skills-that-employers-are-looking-for-in-2022/) that employers are looking for in 2022. [Gen AI skills](https://www.themuse.com/advice/in-demand-generative-ai-skills) data scientists need in 2024.

## OVERVIEW

Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Gen AI have found several applications in business. There is now a foundation of content that students who wish to work in this field need to know and this course is aimed at providing students with a conceptual understanding of the field and its business applications, and a technical toolkit to implement NLP models. The course will include using AWS SageMaker and Bedrock, cloud services for AI/machine learning. Free credits are made available on [AWS Educate](https://aws.amazon.com/education/awseducate/). 

## LEARNING OBJECTIVES

- Explain the business areas in which NLP may be applied. 
- Describe the important concepts and mathematical models for NLP.
- Implement programming languages and toolkits on NLP models for business applications. 
- Learn how to build and deploy NLP models on cloud infrastructure. 

## PREREQUISITES

Students will need an understanding of Linear Algebra (familiarity with matrix multiplication, inverses, determinants, eigensystems, etc.) plus some calculus-based probability, and multivariate calculus. Students should also be able to program in Python and use IDEs such as Jupyter and VSCode.
